Company Announcement (posted by FDA): Golden Valley Produce LLC Recalls Fresh Yellow Onions Because of Possible Health Risk

SALINAS, Calif. — Golden Valley Produce LLC of Salinas, California is voluntarily recalling 25 lb sacks of fresh yellow onions, lot codes GVP-8842 and GVP-8843, because the product has the potential to be contaminated with Escherichia coli O157:H7 (E. coli O157:H7), an organism which can cause severe and sometimes life-threatening illness.

The recalled onions were packed at the company's Golden Valley Packing #2 facility in Salinas, California between June 20, 2026 and July 2, 2026, and were distributed to wholesalers, foodservice distributors, food banks, and institutional customers in California and Nevada.

The recall was initiated after routine environmental sampling by the company and follow-up testing by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) found E. coli O157:H7 in the packing environment. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) is investigating a multistate outbreak of E. coli O157:H7 infections that may be linked to fresh onions; the investigation is ongoing.

Customers who have the recalled onions should not consume, serve, or further distribute them, and should quarantine remaining product pending further instruction. No lot codes other than GVP-8842 and GVP-8843 are affected at this time.

Consumers and institutional customers with questions may contact Golden Valley Produce LLC at (855) 555-0142, Monday through Friday, 8am to 5pm PT, or recalls@goldenvalleyproduce.example.
